## LinkedIn Post — client-approval-workflow

Most agencies treat email as their client approval workflow.

Here is why that fails:
— No versioning. Six people looking at six different file versions.
— No status tracking. You reply all and hope the right person sees it.
— No enforcement. Three stakeholders all think they are the approver.

One design revision. 47 unread messages. Two weeks of follow-ups. Your team has already started the next sprint and the last one is still unapproved.

A functional client approval workflow has four non-negotiables:

→ Clear ownership: one person with budget authority says yes.
→ Single-version truth: one current link, with archived history.
→ Structured feedback: required fields replace vague comments like "make it pop."
→ Documented sign-off: a timestamped confirmation, not a thumbs-up emoji.

A freelance brand designer I track implemented this last quarter. She replaced her email approval chain with a structured three-step process. Client approval cycle: five days → under 24 hours. Bonus revision rounds: gone.

You do not need new software to start fixing this today. Pick one active project. Name the approver. Create one shared link. Add required feedback fields. Document the sign-off.

Agencies that fix their client approval workflow deliver faster, invoice sooner, and protect their margins. The ones that ignore it pay a hidden tax on every project.
