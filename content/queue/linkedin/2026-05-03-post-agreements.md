**Post Body**

In 2019, I watched a $12,000 project dissolve over a voice note.

The client said they wanted the logo "a bit more modern." The agency lead heard "minor tweak." Three weeks later, the client was on round four of revisions, claiming the original request included a full brand refresh.

The agency had no written record. $12,000 in unbilled hours. A designer who nearly quit. A relationship that never recovered.

That is not bad client management. That is documentation failure.

The painful irony is that most scope creep is not malicious. It is memory. What one person recalls as a small ask, another recalls as a major change order. Verbal agreements evaporate the moment the conversation ends.

The agencies that stop repeating this story are not the ones with the strongest legal teams. They are the ones with the fastest documentation habits. A conversation receipt sent in 90 seconds, confirmed with a one-time code, and archived before the next call begins.

If your client agreement process takes longer than brewing coffee, it is too slow.

What is the most expensive scope creep lesson you have learned?
