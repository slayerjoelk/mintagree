## 2. LinkedIn — Founder Journey Post
**Best time:** 8:30–9:30 AM EST (now is ideal)
**Format:** Long-form text post (personal founder story)
**Source blog:** Why we built MintAgree in 90 days

We built MintAgree in 90 days with one developer.

Not because we are exceptional engineers. Because we refused to overengineer.

The stack is intentionally boring. Next.js 16. SQLite via Turso. Tailwind CSS. No Kubernetes. No microservices. No design-by-committee.

I have seen startups spend six months choosing a database and another six months configuring it. We chose SQLite because it is fast, simple, and scales farther than most people think. Turso gives us edge distribution with zero config.

The 3D effects on the landing page look premium, but they are lightweight — built with React Three Fiber and Drei, loading in under two seconds. We did not add them to impress developers. We added them because a prospect told us our old landing page looked like a template.

Every feature we shipped solves a specific problem for a specific customer. Nothing else made the cut.

That is the real lesson: speed comes from focus, not from working harder. If you cannot explain why a feature exists in one sentence, it does not belong in the first 90 days.

What is your rule for saying no to features in early-stage product?
