**Tweet 1 / 5 — Hook**

Scope creep is not a client problem. It is a documentation problem.

I have watched agencies lose $40K+ to "minor tweaks" that turned into full rebuilds.

Every single one started with a voice note or a Slack message that nobody wrote down.

The fix is not saying no more. It is writing it down faster.

---

**Tweet 2 / 5 — Conflict**

Client says "can we make the logo more modern?"

You hear "one-hour tweak."

They mean "full brand refresh."

Without a written confirmation of exactly what was agreed, you are both speaking different languages.

Guess who pays for the translation.

---

**Tweet 3 / 5 — Solution**

Documentation does not mean 12-page contracts.

It means a 90-second conversation receipt.

Scope. Budget. Deadline. Confirmed with a code.

That is the paper trail that protects your margins.

---

**Tweet 4 / 5 — Reframe**

The agency that documents before they start does not lose to scope creep.

They win because they set boundaries before emotions get involved.

Your future self will thank your past self for hitting send on that receipt.
