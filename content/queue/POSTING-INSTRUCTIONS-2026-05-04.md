## 2. LinkedIn — WhatsApp Agreements Post
**Best time:** 8:30–9:30 AM EST (now is ideal)
**Format:** Long-form text post

Clients love WhatsApp. It is fast, informal, and always open.

Agencies tolerate it. It is fast, informal, and impossible to audit.

The problem is not the channel. It is the documentation gap. A client sends a voice note approving a change order. Three weeks later, they have forgotten. You have no record.

I have seen this pattern destroy margin repeatedly. An approved budget in the morning becomes a disputed invoice in the afternoon, all because the agreement lived in a disappearing message thread.

The fix is not banning WhatsApp. That is where the conversation happens. The fix is bridging the gap between conversation and record.

Here is what works:

After the WhatsApp conversation, generate a receipt with the key points: scope, budget, due date, and a reference to the original thread. The client confirms with a one-time code. Under 90 seconds total.

The result is searchable, auditable, and binding. You keep the speed of WhatsApp. You gain the protection of a signed record.

For agencies on thin margins, this is not a convenience. It is survival.

What is your policy on client agreements sent via WhatsApp or text?
