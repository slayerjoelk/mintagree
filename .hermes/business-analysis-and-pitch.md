| Adobe Sign | $12.99/mo | Acrobat ecosystem, PDF muscle | PDF-editing focus, not conversation-to-commitment |

### Category B: Meeting Transcription (raw data, no approval)

| Competitor | Price | What They Do | The Gap |

| Otter.ai | Free / $16.99/mo Pro | Auto-transcribe meetings, AI summaries | Raw transcript, NO structured receipt, NO client sign-off |

| Fathom | Free / $19/mo Premium | AI meeting summaries, highlights | Notetaker tool — no agreement flow, no OTP, no structured bullets |
| Gong | $5k-15k/yr/seat | Revenue intelligence, deal analysis | Enterprise sales only, eye-watering prices, 6-month sales cycles |

### Category C: Project Management (task tracking, not agreement)

| Competitor | Price | What They Do | The Gap |
|-----------|-------|-------------|---------|
| Monday.com | $9/mo/user | Visual PM, dashboards | Building projects, not confirming agreements |
| Asana | $10.99/mo/user | Task management, timelines | "Do this task" vs "We agreed this scope" |
| ClickUp | $7/mo/user | All-in-one PM | Feature bloat, 300+ features nobody asked for |

### Category D: Contract-Lite / Proposals (closest competitors)

| Competitor | Price | What They Do | The Gap |
|-----------|-------|-------------|---------|
| Qwilr | $35/mo/user | Beautiful proposals → agreements | Proposals look great, but START with a template doc — not a conversation |
| Proposify | $49/mo (2 users) | Proposal builder with e-sign | Document-first, not voice-first |
| Better Proposals | $13/mo | Simple proposal tool | Cheapest option but still document-template based |
| Paperform | $20/mo | Forms + payments + e-sign | Form builder, not conversation receipt tool |

### Category E: The One Direct Competitor That Does Exist (barely)

**Supernormal** — "Share meeting notes with action items"
- Free tier available, $10/mo Pro
- Auto-generates meeting notes with action items
- Sends to participants
- BUT: no OTP verification, no receipt format, no scope/budget/due-date structure, no client sign-off
- They're in the "better notetaker" camp, not the "agreement receipt" camp

### THE FUNDAMENTAL GAP

Every competitor attacks from one direction:
- E-sign tools → "Here's a legal contract to sign" (heavy, slow, formal)
- Transcript tools → "Here's everything that was said" (unstructured, no sign-off)
- PM tools → "Here are your tasks" (execution, not agreement)
- Proposal tools → "Here's a beautiful document" (document-first, not conversation-first)

**NOBODY does: "Here's what we just agreed on the call — confirm with a code."**

## 2. AGREEMINT'S UNFAIR ADVANTAGE

### The "90-Second Receipt" Moat

AgreeMint doesn't compete with DocuSign or Otter — it occupies the space BETWEEN them.

| DocuSign | AgreeMint | Otter |
|----------|-----------|-------|
| Legal contract | **Conversation receipt** | Raw transcript |
| 20-page PDF | **6 bullet points** | 8-page transcript |
| "Sign here x7" | **"Enter this code"** | Nobody signs anything |
| 3 days to sign | **90 seconds** | Instant (but useless) |
| Lawyers involved | **No lawyers needed** | No structure at all |
| $10/mo | **TBD** | $16.99/mo |

### The Behavioral Hook

The moment AFTER a client call is when agreement is freshest and disputes are born. Every service business owner has experienced:

1. "I thought we agreed on 3 rounds of revisions, not 5"
2. "You said it would be $1,500, not $2,200"
3. "When did we discuss the deadline being next week?"

AgreeMint captures agreement at peak clarity — right after the call. The OTP is the "agree" moment. It's lightweight enough that clients actually use it, structured enough that it prevents disputes.

### Network Effects (Latent)

When a client receives their first AgreeMint receipt and signs off in seconds, they've experienced the workflow. If they run their own service business, they want it for THEIR clients. Every receipt sent is a product demo to a potential new customer.

## 5. WHAT WE'RE MISSING (And Must Build)

### Critical Gaps vs Competitors

1. **No calendar integration** — Otter/Fireflies auto-join meetings. AgreeMint requires manual entry. Add Google/Microsoft Calendar OAuth to auto-detect client calls and pre-fill receipts.

2. **No analytics dashboard** — Gong/Chorus show deal intelligence. Add: receipt open rate, time-to-sign, dispute rate, most active clients, receipt volume trends. This is the upgrade hook that converts Starter → Pro.

3. **No team collaboration** — The "Assignments" feature is listed but not built. Add: assign bullet items to teammates, due-date notifications, team receipt view.

4. **No CRM integration** — HubSpot/Salesforce connectors. Even a simple Zapier webhook would unlock 10x distribution.

5. **No receipt templates library** — "Playbooks" is listed but the templates API exists without a public library. Build 10+ pre-loaded industry templates (Agency, Consulting, MSP, Construction, Legal, Creative, etc.)

6. **No SSO / enterprise features** — Missing for Enterprise tier: SAML, audit logs, custom domains, API keys.

7. **No mobile app** — The dashboard is mobile-responsive but no push notifications. PWA wrapper would add "receipt signed" push alerts.

8. **No payment integration** — PayFast endpoint doesn't exist. Without payments, there's no business.

### What to Build NOW (Priority Order)

1. **PayFast endpoint** — revenue-critical, blocking
2. **Calendar integration** — biggest UX gap, pre-fills receipts from meetings
3. **Analytics dashboard** — the upgrade hook, justifies Pro pricing
4. **Template library** — reduces onboarding friction, shows industry expertise
5. **CRM webhook/Zapier** — distribution multiplier

## 6. THE PITCH

Here's how this gets sold — to investors, customers, and the market.

### Elevator Pitch (10 seconds)

"AgreeMint turns the end of every client call into a signed receipt. 90 seconds. 6 bullets. One code. Disputes drop. Projects start aligned."

### Problem Statement

Every day, 50 million client calls happen globally. After each one, there's a verbal agreement on scope, budget, and deadlines. But nothing is documented. The gap between "we discussed it" and "signed contract" is where disputes are born — scope creep, missed deadlines, budget overruns, "I thought we agreed on..."

Existing tools fail here:
- DocuSign is for lawyers (too heavy, takes days)
- Otter is for notetakers (captures everything, confirms nothing)
- Monday is for managers (tracks tasks, not agreements)

The result? Service businesses lose 20-40% of project margin to misalignment and rework. Freelancers spend 8 hours/week managing scope creep. Agencies burn client relationships over undocumented verbal changes.

### Solution

AgreeMint fills the gap with a **conversation receipt** — a lightweight, 6-bullet summary sent immediately after a call. The client enters a one-time code to acknowledge. It takes 90 seconds total.

It's a **contract-lite** — between a transcript and a legal contract. Structured enough to prevent disputes. Lightweight enough that clients actually use it.

### Market Timing (Why Now)

1. **Remote-first is permanent** — 58% of Americans work remotely at least part-time. Client calls happen on Zoom, not in boardrooms. The "verbal handshake" moment is now invisible — there's no body language, no paper trail, no assistant taking notes.

2. **AI transcription is solved** — Otter, Fireflies, and Fathom have trained the market to expect call data. But they stopped at transcription. The NEXT step — turning transcription into agreement — is completely unclaimed.

3. **Freelancer economy is exploding** — 73M+ US freelancers by 2028 (Statista). The tools they use (Calendly for scheduling, Stripe for payments, Notion for notes) don't cover the agreement gap. AgreeMint is the missing piece.

4. **eSignature market is saturated** — DocuSign owns 70% share. The play is NOT to compete on eSign — it's to define a new category ("contract-lite agreement receipts") that eSign tools don't serve and never will.
